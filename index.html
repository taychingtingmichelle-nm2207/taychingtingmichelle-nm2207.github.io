In this day and age, social media has become almost a necessity for us. Furthermore, technology has advanced so rapidly that we even carry social media within our smart phones no matter where we are on the go. By definition, social media refers to the means of interactions among people in which they create, share, and/or exchange information and ideas in virtual communities and networks. Social media has become very essential in our daily lives for most of us.

This data story seeks to provide you with more knowledge regarding the usage, impact, pros and cons of social media.




Social Media Usage




Regional Social Media Users Worldwide (2022)

According to Statista, in 2022, Eastern Asia accounted for over 1.1 billion social media users, the majority of which were located in China. Additionally, Southern Asia had a total of 847 million social media users, whilst central Africa was home to just over 21 million users. And, if we generalise the data just by looking at the main 4 regions, namely Asia, Pacific, America, Europe, Africa, the proportion shows that Asia alone constitute 60.8% of all social media users worldwide. Average daily usage on Social Media worldwide (2022 Q2)

Based on the daily average screen-time (in 2022 Q2, May-Aug), TikTok champions every other platforms with a daily average screen time of 95 minutes. In a another survey done more recently in January 2023, results showed that FaceBook was in fact regarded the most popular social media platforms of all time. However, TikTok still manages to beat FaceBook in terms of screen-time. This also means that being the most popular social media platform does not guarantee a high average screen-time in comparison to others.





Growth of Instagram, Facebook, TikTok (2018 to 2022)

Comparing the growth of Instagram, Facebook and TikTok, we can see that Facebook still tops the charts based on the absolute number of users during this period. On the other hand, Instagram is still ahead of TikTok, having more annual users. Instagram and TikTok both are increasing at a steady and similar rate. However, the growth trend for Facebook can be seen to be going stagnant from 2021 onwards as the growth of users from 2021 to 2022 was merely 23 million.

Uses of Social Media (2023)

According to a recent survey, the largest reason people use social media is to connect with their friends and family. As expected, social media is designed mainly for this purpose. From profiles, messaging, and commenting options to sharing milestones and daily updates, there's very little about an individual (or even a business) that doesn't eventually find its way onto social media. Given that social media is visual content-heavy, it is fairly easy to consume, and offers infinite scrolling, its entertainment value is extremely high.

Cyber Bullying

Cyberbullying is one of most harmful side of social media and it's a form of online harrassment. It can take on many forms which include spreading rumors, lies, impersonation and even cancel culture. Now, let's also take a look at the following data. The chart below shows data regarding about parental awareness of which digital platforms were used for cyber bullying, sorted by region. The survey showed that 76% of all responding parents in Latin America were aware of social media as a platform for cyber bullying children. The global average knowledge about social media as a cyber bullying medium was 65%.





Source:Statista





Source: Combined Datasets from Statista


According to Statista, 3 surveys (as of Apr 2018) were done in China, India and Malaysia, the results showed that most children in India and Malaysia were cyber bullied through social networks while children in China were mostly cyber bullied through mobile. We can also observe that the likelihood of children in china getting cyber bullied through online messaging and online chat rooms are also very close to mobile. Unlike India and Malaysia, children in China do not experience cyber bullying through social networks.





Source: Statista


Source: Statista


According to SingleCare, The top three countries with the highest rate of cyber bullying are India, Brazil and the United States. Focusing on the prevelance of cyber bullying in United States, we can refer to the polar and bar charts above. We can see that most cyber bullying victims are contributed by transgender as depicted in the polar chart. In addition, the horizontal bar chart shows that most students in the U.S mostly experienced receiving mean or hurtful comments from their account online and this result was also published by the Cyberbullying Research Center.


Looking at adults now, let us observe The data results on the right. This results are also published by Pew Research Center. This survey was done in September 2020 with adults responses within the United States. Approximately two thirds of people aged 30 or under have experienced some form of online harassment. Overall, younger adults were the most targeted age group for both less and more severe forms of abuse in online settings, as well as being the only group in which the majority of people reported experiencing online abuse. Of the overall 64% of online harassment victims aged under 30 years, only 16% reported less severe forms of harassment, with the majority of respondents saying they had been targeted with more severe online harassment. Adults aged 30 to 49 are also more likely to be affected by more severe forms of online harassment as opposed to less severe behaviours.

Mental Health & Emotional Influences

The next significant effect social media has on the general public today is no other than mental health, as well as our emotional state. When you see someone post about how great their life is online, some people can't help but to compare themselves with others and doing that makes them feel less aobut themselves. Another quick example is how people also get into an addiction with the online community that they no longer choose to emerse themselves in authentic social experiences, leading to depression and loneliness, in other words, isolation.

According to the findings, 35% of teenagers with low social-emotional well-being reported to have been cyber bullied when using social media, while in comparison only five percent of teenagers with high social-emotional well-being stated similarly.

In addition, according to a global survey conducted in 2021, 36% of respondents belonging to Generation Z felt that social media usage had a negative impact on social wellbeing. The feeling that social media negatively affected wellbeing declined with age, and just 21 percent of Generation X respondents felt that social media harmed social wellbeing. This statistic may not be that shocking to many as social media are mainly contributed by Gen Z to begin with.
