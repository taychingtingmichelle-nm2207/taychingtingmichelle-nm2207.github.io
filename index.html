A data story

by tay ching ting michelle


Social media has revolutionised the way people communicate, connect and consume information today. It has become an integral part of our daily lives and transformed how businesses and organisations interact with customers, helping them reach wider audiences and build stronger relationships. Social media has made news, entertainment and even personal interactions accessible with a smartphone and this convenience is no doubt one of the best inventions of human kind. However, the use of social media also yields negative consequences. To name a few, misinformation, cyberbullying, negative mental health effects and privacy concerns.

This data story seeks to provide you with a more updated understanding in the usage and impact that social media has on us today.




Social Media Usage




Regional Social Media Users Worldwide (2022)

In 2022, Eastern Asia accounted for over 1.1 billion social media users, the majority of which were located in China. Additionally, Southern Asia had a total of 847 million social media users, whilst central Africa was home to just over 21 million users. And, if we generalise the data just by looking at the main 4 regions, namely Asia, Pacific, America, Europe, Africa, the proportion shows that Asia alone constitute 60.8% of all social media users worldwide. Average daily usage on Social Media worldwide (2022 Q2)

Based on the daily average screen-time (in 2022 Q2, May-Aug), TikTok champions every other platforms with a daily average screen time of 95 minutes. In a another survey done more recently in January 2023, results showed that FaceBook was in fact regarded the most popular social media platforms of all time. However, TikTok still manages to beat FaceBook in terms of screen-time. This also means that being the most popular social media platform does not guarantee a high average screen-time in comparison to others.





Growth of Instagram, Facebook, TikTok (2018 to 2022)

Comparing the growth of Instagram, Facebook and TikTok, we can see that Facebook still tops the charts based on the absolute number of users during this period. On the other hand, Instagram is still ahead of TikTok, having more annual users. Instagram and TikTok both are increasing at a steady and similar rate. However, the growth trend for Facebook can be seen to be going stagnant from 2021 onwards as the growth of users from 2021 to 2022 was merely 23 million.

Uses of Social Media (2023)

According to a recent survey, the largest reason people use social media is to connect with their friends and family. As expected, social media is designed mainly for this purpose. From profiles, messaging, and commenting options to sharing milestones and daily updates, there's very little about an individual (or even a business) that doesn't eventually find its way onto social media. Given that social media is visual content-heavy, it is fairly easy to consume, and offers infinite scrolling, its entertainment value is extremely high.

Cyber Bullying

Cyberbullying is one of most harmful side of social media and it's a form of online harrassment. It can take on many forms which include spreading rumors, lies, impersonation and even cancel culture. Now, let's also take a look at the following data. The chart below shows data regarding about parental awareness of which digital platforms were used for cyber bullying, sorted by region. The survey showed that 76% of all responding parents in Latin America were aware of social media as a platform for cyber bullying children. The global average knowledge about social media as a cyber bullying medium was 65%.





Source:Statista





Source: Combined Datasets from Statista


Based on 3 surveys (Apr 2018) that were done in China, India and Malaysia, the results showed that most children in India and Malaysia were cyber bullied through social networks while children in China were mostly cyber bullied through mobile. We can also observe that the likelihood of children in china getting cyber bullied through online messaging and online chat rooms are also very close to mobile. Unlike India and Malaysia, there are relatively few children in China that experience cyber bullying through social networks.





Source: Statista


Source: Statista


According to SingleCare, The top three countries with the highest rate of cyber bullying are India, Brazil and the United States. Focusing on the prevelance of cyber bullying in United States, we can see that most cyber bullying victims are contributed by transgender persons as depicted in the polar chart. In addition, the horizontal bar chart shows that most students in the U.S mostly experienced receiving mean or hurtful comments from their account online and this result was also published by the Cyberbullying Research Center.


When looking at Cyberbullying from the adults experience, The results of a survey (Sep 2020) that was published by Pew Research Center tells us that approximately two thirds of people aged 30 or under have experienced some form of online harassment. Overall, younger adults were the most targeted age group for both less and more severe forms of abuse in online settings, as well as being the only group in which the majority of people reported experiencing online abuse. Of the overall 64% of online harassment victims aged under 30 years, only 16% reported less severe forms of harassment, with the majority of respondents saying they had been targeted with more severe online harassment. Adults aged 30 to 49 are also more likely to be affected by more severe forms of online harassment as opposed to less severe behaviours.



Source: Statista









Mental Health & Emotional Influences

The next significant effect social media has on the general public today is none other than mental health. Some people can't help but to compare themselves with what their peers put online. Social media then becomes a platform where you develop a certain set of norms or standards and and one such example can be women's current beauty standard. Some may even develop social media addiction and no longer seek authentic social interaction, which can eventually lead to depression and isolation.













Source: Reset.stck.me


















Source:Statista

Referring to the radar chart above, 35% of teenagers with low social-emotional well-being reported to have been cyber bullied when using social media, while in comparison only 5% of teenagers with high social-emotional well-being stated similarly. This is naturally expected as people who are likely to feel more negative are also likely to be more sensitive towards social media effects. A large percentage of those who have low social-emotional wellbeing also feel excluded.



Source: Statista


And in a global survey conducted in 2021, 36% of respondents belonging to Generation Z felt that social media usage had a negative impact on social wellbeing. The feeling that social media negatively affected wellbeing declined with age, and just 21% of Generation X respondents felt that social media harmed social wellbeing. This statistic may not be that shocking to many as social media are mainly contributed by Gen Z to begin with. This would naturally imply that the likelihood for those to feel lonely are no other than Gen Zs. Furthermore, Gen Zs also grew up at a time where technology had advanced really rapidly.





In another survey with a relatively small sample size of 272, adults aged 18-25 who were active users on social media rarely felt that social media was a cause of sleep deprivation. However, the reliability of the result could still be questionable due to the small sample size.
